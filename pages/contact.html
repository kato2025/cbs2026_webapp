Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can attend CBS 2026?

CBS 2026 is open to all researchers, academics, postgraduate students, policymakers, industry professionals, and anyone with an interest in biological sciences. Participants from all countries are welcome in-person or virtually.

Is virtual attendance available?

Yes. CBS 2026 offers a hybrid format. Virtual attendees will have live access to keynote sessions and selected parallel sessions. Platform details will be shared to registered participants closer to the event.

Can students submit abstracts?

Absolutely. MSc and PhD students are strongly encouraged to submit. There will be a dedicated student poster competition with prizes. Undergraduates are welcome as co-authors or attendees.

Will there be a proceedings publication?

Yes. Accepted abstracts will be published in the CBS 2026 Book of Abstracts. Full papers may be considered for a peer-reviewed journal. Details will be communicated to accepted authors.

How do I get to Buea from Douala Airport?

Buea is approximately 70 km from Douala International Airport (1.5–2 hours by road). Taxis, agency buses, and shared vehicles are available. Transport guidance will be provided to registered participants.

I need a visa invitation letter. Who do I contact?

Registered participants requiring a formal invitation letter for visa purposes should email kato.namuene@ubuea.cm with their full name, institution, and passport details. Allow at least 6 weeks for processing.

What accommodation options are available?

A curated list of recommended hotels and guesthouses at various price points will be provided to all registered participants. Buea offers good options within minutes of the University campus.

What is the maximum abstract length?

Abstracts must not exceed 300 words. They should follow a structured format: Background, Methods, Results, and Conclusion. No tables, figures, or references are permitted within the abstract text.
